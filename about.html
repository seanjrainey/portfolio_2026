Background

Over the past decade, I've led brand and website engagements across technology, health, property development, and investment.

I design brand identities. I design websites. And I build them. One person accountable for the entire result — no handoffs, no gap between what was designed and what gets built.

Clients come to me when they want senior thinking without agency layers. A brand isn't a logo and a website isn't a brochure — both are business tools, and both should work as hard as the team behind them.

Why I work this way

I'm Irish-born, Vancouver-based, and three years ago I made a decision: do this one way — start to finish, one person, full ownership.

There's something about taking a brand from nothing — the first conversation about who you are and what you're trying to say — all the way through to a finished website that earns trust on your behalf. Every touchpoint considered. No one to blame if it's wrong, no one to share the credit if it's right. That accountability is the point.

It's not the most scalable model. But it's the one that produces the work I'm proud of.

How I work

I keep things simple. One relationship, one point of contact, one person who owns the outcome from start to finish.

I don't subcontract. I don't disappear between milestones. And I don't deliver work that needs someone else to explain it.
